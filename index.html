Emma Witheridge

Band 6 SACT Nurse

Summary

After qualifying in 2016 with a first, BSc Hons degree in adult nursing I began my nursing career on a respiratory ward in Bournemouth. Working both on the ward and within the acute lung unit I developed core skills as well as those specific to the speciality including chest drain management and non-invasive ventilation (CPAP, BIPAP), which were an important and enjoyable stepping stone in my career. During this time I also developed an interest in palliative care, which was strengthened when I undertook the QELCA (Quality End of Life Care for All) course and feel I thrive best in an environment that facilitates the delivery of high quality patient-centred care.

Owing to my fascination with Chinese culture, in 2017 I took a career break to undertake a 5 month scholarship In China and achieved HSK level 4 in Mandarin Chinese.

In 2019 I left Bournemouth and relocated to Oxford where I have since worked as a chemotherapy nurse on the day treatment unit (DTU) at the Churchill hospital. Since joining DTU and completing the SACT Administration and Patient Care course at Oxford Brookes University, I have gained valuable experience in cancer care across both the haematology and oncology sides of the department and developed proficiency in a number of clinical skills including (but not limited to): pre-treatment assessments, SACT administration, administration of blood products and venesection. I later undertook further studies at level 7, whilst working full-time, to aid with my professional development and in March 2023 I was awarded a Post Graduate Certificate (PG Cert) in Cancer Care.

Skills

  • Conducting patient assessments prior to:
  • Administation of Systematic Anti-Cancer Treatments (IV, SC, IM)
  • Venepuncture and Cannulation
  • Venesections
  • PICC line care and management
  • PICC line removal
  • Accessing and using Portacaths
  • Administration of blood products (Red blood cells and platelets)
  • Co-ordination of ward area
  • Basic Life Support
  • Managing adverse reactions to SACT
